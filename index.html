About Me

I work full-time in logistics operations, where I deal with real-world problems, routing constraints, and constantly changing requirements. Practical experience has taught me that elegant solutions only matter if they survive reality.

Alongside work, I study Engineering at the Open University, combining theoretical foundations with daily exposure to complex operational systems. Concepts from coursework often appear in practice, in imperfect and constrained forms.

I'm building a modular transport management system by developing small, independent services - routing, scheduling, customer management - that solve specific problems on their own and can later be composed into a larger system.

This site documents that process: experiments, working systems, and lessons learned along the way.
